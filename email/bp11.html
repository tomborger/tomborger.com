It's speed art — and you're the patron.

See the Twin Cities’ best playwrights, directors, and actors turn your idea into a play and perform it — live, on stage, all in one night. Enjoy food and drinks while helping us commemorate 39 years of supporting the American theater.

Join co-hosts Producing Artistic Director Jeremy B. Cohen and treasured Twin Cities performer T. Mychael Rambo on May 14th at 7 pm for BIDS & PIECES — a party only the Playwrights' Center could throw.

 
   
 

1. We go first.

During the evening you’ll see two brand new short plays written especially for this event by internationally acclaimed playwright Lee Blessing and bound-for-Broadway Lisa D’Amour, commissioned by the Playwrights’ Center and a few of our friends.

2. Then it's your turn.

Choose an "auction playwright" below. Bring a quirky topic or twisted plotline — or better yet, that friend who ought to have a play written about them! In our live auction, bid, win, and have your play performed by some of the best actors in town.
 
     
     
 
Belly up to the New Play Bar with some dramatic ingredients, and watch as one of our writers shakes and stirs them into a short play jotted on a cocktail napkin for just $39.

Additional auction items

The play's not your thing? Stake your claim to our other great auction items, including theater packages in New York, Chicago, and Minneapolis; a local night out with a Twin Cities theater luminary; or a week in a vacation home in western Colorado.

Lee Blessing

Lee Blessing is the author of A Walk in the Woods, which ran on Broadway and in London’s West End. His prize-winning plays include Eleemosynary, A Body of Water, Two Rooms, Cobb and Going to St. Ives among others. Portland’s Profile Theatre is currently doing a full season of his plays.
 
     
 
 

Lisa D'Amour

A Playwrights’ Center Core Alum whose work has been presented by Salvage Vanguard Theater, Infernal Bridegroom Productions, the Walker Arts Center, Red Eye Theater, Crowded Fire Theater, Children’s Theater Company, and many more. Her play Detroit premiered at Steppenwolf Theatre in 2010 and will arrive on Broadway in fall 2011.
 
     
 
  Barbara Field
Her heroes are the late Charles Nolte, the late Michael Langham and the late Garland Wright … beware!
     
  Aditi Brennan Kapil
Aditi is inclined toward plays with multiple languages, irritable characters, bad jokes, talking fruit, and/or time travel. She would like to meet an idea that also enjoys any or all of these activities. If we’re a match, it may not end well (in fact it’s dramatically preferable that it not) but the ride will sure be entertaining.
     
  Allison Moore
If you’re looking for pieces about bad horror movies, shady southerners, pushy mothers or relentless self-improvers, Allison’s your gal.
     
  Dominic Orlando
Dominic has been dramatizing supervillains and vast underground criminal networks lately, and is an excellent choice for ideas or bidders that fall into either category.
     
  Mat Smart
If you’re looking for a play about love, baseball, the endless sorrow that only comes from a) being a Cubs fan or b) loving and losing a beautiful woman, or anything in between — Mat’s your playwright for the night.
